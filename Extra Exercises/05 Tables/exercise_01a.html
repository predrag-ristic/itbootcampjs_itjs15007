Rome’s Top Tourist Attractions

More than 15.2 million tourists visited Rome in 2018, an increase of 1.1 million on the number of visitors in 2017. Here are some of the things that shouldn't be missed in the Italian capital.

The Colosseum was built in 72 A.D. and hosted many gladiator fights. Some say that it was also filled with water for ship battles, but experts don’t know if it definitely was. As many as 6 million tourists now visit the Colosseum every year.

The Pantheon was built as a Roman temple, but has been a Christian church since 609 A.D. and has become an important tourist attraction. It's a great example of a building from ancient Rome because even after almost 2,000 years it's not been as badly damaged as other famous Roman buildings.

The Vatican city is located in Rome, but it is really the world’s smallest country and the center of the Roman Catholic church. It has 11 museums and one of the world’s largest private art collections. It is also home to the Sistine chapel, where artist Michelangelo painted its famous ceiling.

Anyone visiting Rome must try the local food. Pasta meals made with lots of cheese like carbonara or cacio e pepe are popular with both Italians and tourists. Suppli are rice balls that can have cheese, meat and tomato in them. For something sweet, try maritozzo, a bread served with cream that is often enjoyed with coffee.

My Two Favorite Places

The Trevi Fountain is an 18th-century fountain in the Trevi district in Rome, Italy, designed by Italian architect Nicola Salvi and completed by Giuseppe Pannini and several others. Standing 26.3 metres (86 ft) high and 49.15 metres (161.3 ft) wide,[1] it is the largest Baroque fountain in the city and one of the most famous fountains in the world.

The Mausoleum of Hadrian, usually known as Castel Sant'Angelo is a towering cylindrical building in Parco Adriano, Rome, Italy. It was initially commissioned by the Roman Emperor Hadrian as a mausoleum for himself and his family. The building was later used by the popes as a fortress and castle, and is now a museum. The structure was once the tallest building in Rome.
